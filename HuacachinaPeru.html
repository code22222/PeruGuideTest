My Review

Huacachina offers an incredible and unique experience with sand skiing, which is unlike anything you'd find elsewhere. However, beyond this activity, the city itself doesn't have much more to offer, so I wouldn't stay here for more than a day and a half.

Itinerary

Day 17

  • Booked sand skiing for later in the day and then walked around the lake and sand dunes
  • Tanned, listened to music, and swam in the pool at the hostel
  • Went sand-skiing and dune buggy tour with amazing sunset views
  • Socialized and partied at the hostel, meeting new people and having a great final night in Peru

Day 18

  • Checked out early, explored a local restaurant, and traveled by bus from Huacachina to Lima (5 hour bus)
  • Ate Ceviche in Lima before going to the airport
  • Flight from LIM to EWR (Home!)

Excursions & Activities

Sand-Skiing

Price: $25
  • This was a surreal, bucket list experience that I am so happy I was able to do. It was so unique skiing on sand, and there was definitely an uncomfortable adjustment period at first, but it was so much fun! I booked the tour the same day from one of the many local vendors in the town, and had time to practice skiing before the “tour” started. Although it was a hassle carrying the skiing equipment up to the dunes, from there we rode in a sand buggy which carried the skis as we rode through the Peruvian desert. The sand buggy ride was an experience in itself, with the driver making rollercoaster-esq maneuvers which just added to the thrill. After sand skiing down a couple hills, we watched a beautiful sunset over the sand dunes. I definitely recommend sand skiing or boogie boarding down the sand dunes as it is an amazing and unique experience.
