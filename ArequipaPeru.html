My Review

Arequipa was a city I really enjoyed, especially for its stunning views of the surrounding volcanoes and mountains. I would have liked to stay a day or two longer to fully explore these natural wonders, as my stay was just long enough to get a feel for the city but not quite enough to venture out into the mountains and volcanoes. I planned it this way thinking I would recover from my exhausting Machu Picchu trek in Arequipa, but by the time I arrieved, I was already recovered and would have enjoyed participating in a volcano hike or other active excursion Arequipa would offer.

Excursions & Activities

Colca Canyon Full Day Tour

Price: $38
  • This tour starts early in the morning with a beautiful sunrise and a view of 3 active volcanoes. From there, the van traverses the Colca Canyon, stopping at various points for picturesque views of all angles of the amazing canyon. The third largest canyon in the world, Colca Canyon is home to the condor, the 2nd largest bird in the world. Throughout the trip, you will get a chance to see these beautiful birds soar through the canyon as you learn the history of the land. There is an hour of “free time” to do optional activities such as ATV riding, ziplining, hot springs, kayaking, and more. At the end of the tour, there is a view of a preservation for alpacas and vicunas, as well as other Andean wildlife. Overall the views of the tour were incredible, and I think it is better to just do a one-day tour rather than a multi-day hike of the canyon.

Rock Climbing Tour

Price: $30
  • I booked this excursion through my hostel (Flying Dog Hostel of Arequipa) and it was definitely a cool experience. It was my first time rock climbing (other than indoor bouldering), but I had a blast learning and challenging myself. This was definitely a very strenuous activity and my fingers and arms were exhausted by the end, but it was worth it!
