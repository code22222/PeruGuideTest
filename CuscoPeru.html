My Review

Cusco is rich in culture and heritage, making it my favorite city in Peru. The cobblestone streets, terracotta roofs, unique food, and vibrant atmosphere never gets old. Cusco also serves as the perfect base for exploring iconic sites like Machu Picchu, the Sacred Valley, and Rainbow Mountain. I enjoyed my time here immensely and wouldn't change the number of days I stayed.

Itinerary

Pre-Machu Picchu

Day 1

  • Flight from LIM (Lima) to CUZ (Cusco) midday
  • Arrived in Cusco hostel via Uber and went to explore the city
  • Bought altitude sickness pills, coca leaf candy, and water which helped subdue the effects of Cusco's high elevation

Day 2

  • Joined a free walking tour offered through the hostel and explored various points of the city
  • Explored the city solo, ending at the Jesus statue for a breathtaking sunset and panoramic view

Day 3

  • Booked an ATV tour through the hostel and enjoyed amazing views of the Sacred Valley and Salt Mines
  • Attended the Machu Picchu Tour briefing to learn important details about the start of my 5 day trek tomorrow

Post-Machu Picchu

Day 4

  • Spent the day relaxing and recovering after the exhausting (but worth it) Machu Picchu Trek
  • Handled cash issues and resolved hostel and trip payments

Day 5

  • Early start for Rainbow Mountain where we bussed through the Peruvian countryside
  • Hiked to the top of Rainbow Mountain for the scenic views and got more pictures with alpacas
  • Bought souvenirs and even tried the local delicacy: Guinea Pig (Cuy)
  • The taste was very odd but the texture resembles chicken. A weird experience for sure but definitely worth a try if you visit!
  • 10 hour bus ride from Cusco to Arequipa (night bus)

Excursions & Activities

ATV Tour of Salt Mines & Sacred Valley

Price: $49
  • I booked through my hostel (Viajero Kokopelli) but the price was the same as online. I definitely recommend it! The views of the sacred valley and salt mines were great, it was fun riding the atvs, and we learned about the unique agricultural and salt mining techniques

Salkantay Trek to Machu Picchu

Price: $320
  • This 5 day trek was one of the greatest experiences of my life and well worth the money. The cost includes 3 meals a day, sleeping accommodations, an experienced and bi-lingual tour guide, mule service to transport bags, tickets to Machu Picchu, and transportation to the start of the trek and to return back to Cusco. From climbing the Salkantay Mountain, to traversing the rainforest, this trek ends at the jaw-dropping wonder of the world: Machu Picchu. Make sure to book this in advance to secure a ticket to Machu Picchu and reserve a good circuit around Machu Picchu (circuit 1&2 have more picture-esq views than 3&4). However, it is possible to book last minute from this provider or others since there are trips starting every day
